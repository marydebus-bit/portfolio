Overview

Launching a professional
funding operation from day one

A newly formed grants team at a space life science research institute needed a complete funding operation designed and built before it ever made its first submission. The institute's research program spans four areas: space biology, radiation biology, reproductive biology, and research infrastructure, each with its own funder landscape, program cycles, and proposal requirements.

Starting fresh is an advantage: no legacy processes to untangle, no inherited workarounds. But it also means every system decision carries forward. The goal was to build something the team could trust, grow into, and hand off to future members without losing institutional knowledge.

The Approach

Three interlocking layers,
one continuous pipeline

Rather than building a single tracker, the system was designed in three interdependent layers: a strategic framework defining what to pursue and why, a live tracking infrastructure managing every opportunity from discovery to submission, and a team playbook establishing shared protocols from the start.

Research Alignment Framework

Four research focus areas serve as the filter for every funding opportunity considered, ensuring the team pursues only what the institute can credibly win.

Funding Strategy Matrix

A three-horizon timeline maps specific funders to each research area across short, mid, and long-term windows. All three tracks run simultaneously, not sequentially.

Operational Tracking System

A four-tab Google Sheet with automated pipeline logic, color-coded urgency alerts, program officer relationship logging, and a live Pipeline Summary dashboard requiring no manual updating.

Team Playbook & Training Suite

A full Funding Operations Playbook, column-by-column user guide, and a one-page cheat sheet giving every team member a clear protocol and shared language from the first day.

System Design

A 7-stage proposal pipeline
with built-in accountability

Every funding opportunity moves through a standardized pipeline. Each stage has a defined meaning, a color code for at-a-glance status, an assigned owner, and a next action with a clear deadline. Updating the Stage column is the only action required to keep the pipeline and dashboard current.
